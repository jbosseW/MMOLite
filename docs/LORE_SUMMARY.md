# Age After the Atlas — World Lore Summary

## Quick Reference

---

### The World at a Glance

Year 500 Post-Atlas. Five hundred years ago, a Dominion warrior carried a divine weapon called Heaven's Atlas into the desert oasis city of Calidar — the joint capital of the Elven and Dark Elven peoples on the continent of Fortuna — and activated it. In a hot flash of white light and sickly green fire, the most beautiful city on the continent became fifty miles of green glass, hot ash, and fluctuating magma. The entirety of both Elven sub-peoples within the city were gone before the smoke rose.

A Dark Elven god placed a punishment on the warrior, forced him into a spatial rift at the epicenter, and moved that rift to the heart of the old human capital as a warning to all its arrogance. The warrior has been inside it ever since. He has been inside it for five hundred years. He has divine fragments stitched into his limbs, the remnants of gods he defeated on the rift's infinite floors. He is not dead. He is trying to find Helios.

He will never find Helios. Helios — the demi-god at the center of the Holy Dominion's theology, son of a divine lord and a mortal woman — is sealed below the Dominion's capital, barely alive, his essence siphoned continuously by the Cardinals to power artifacts of war. He has not been conscious in centuries.

The rift at the capital has been growing for five hundred years. New rifts are forming across Fortuna. The thing the Dominion calls a lich — which is the warrior, changed by centuries of divine punishment and the fragments of defeated gods — is sending necromancers to tear the fabric of the world wider. He wants out. He wants to reach his god. He does not know what has happened to his god.

Eight races share Fortuna under the Dominion's theological authority. None of them chose this arrangement. The Adventure Guild — the only institution all eight races have signed onto — is the world's best and only real response to the rifts. You find yourself in one of the smaller towns outside the capital where a new rift has just formed. Enter it. Find out what started this. Understand: the man inside has been trying to get out for five hundred years. He has not stopped because he cannot stop. The artifact in his chest will not let him.

### The Central Mystery

**Who is the Lich?** A Dominion warrior — righteous by every measure his culture possessed, chosen personally by the Prophet-King — who was sent to deploy Heaven's Atlas against Calidar. A Dark Elven god implanted a malicious artifact in his chest and forced him into the Rift at the epicenter. The warrior spent what felt like aeons on the Rift's shifting floors, fighting hollow replica beings across infinite configurations. He was tasked with retrieving five divine souls. He retrieved them. The god took them, tore the artifact from his chest, and told him this was his eternal torment. The god moved the Rift to the old human capital and departed. The warrior has been inside it ever since, with divine fragments from five defeated gods distributed across his limbs and skull. He cannot die. He cannot leave by conventional means. He is trying to reach Helios. He is trying to understand what he did. The answer to what he did is available. The answer to where Helios is is not something he will find.

**What is Helios?** A demi-god. Son of a divine lord who laid eyes on a mortal woman, and thus Helios was born — powerful enough to be a genuine deity, not powerful enough to resist being sealed. The Atlas took a massive toll: it contained one of Helios's own organs, and the detonation nearly destroyed him. The Cardinals who discovered this in Year 3 made a decision that their successors have maintained for five centuries: seal Helios below the Cathedral, use the residual divine essence as a power source, and maintain the Doctrine's claim that Helios is the supreme deity of all Fortuna. The religion is now sustained by a god who cannot respond to prayer. The divine warmth that the Doctrine's faithful feel in their services is not Helios. It is the memory of Helios, reflected back through architectural choices and centuries of ritual practice. Helios himself has not been conscious for a very long time.

**What are the new rifts?** The lich pushing slivers of himself outward through the primary Rift, sending necromancers to tear the fabric of Fortuna wherever his influence can reach. Each new rift is a door he is trying to open. He is not trying to conquer Fortuna. He is trying to find Helios. Every rift that opens is him reaching through the wall, looking for a signal that never comes.

**What happens if he succeeds in widening the rifts enough to emerge?** The divine fragments in his body — five gods' worth of residual power distributed across his limbs — would be in the physical world. What emerges from the Rift would not be the man who went in. It would be something that has spent five centuries being reshaped by divine punishment, the Rift's impossible geometries, and the fragments of defeated gods. Whether that thing can be reasoned with, whether it still contains the soldier who believed in his mission, is the question no one has had to answer yet.

### The Eight Playable Races

**Humans (The Holy Dominion).** Population approximately 1.5 million. Governed by a theocratic empire built around the worship of Helios and the Helios Doctrine, which holds that humanity is divinely mandated to administer all other peoples of Fortuna. Lifespan 70-90 years. The Dominion controls the central plains, the largest agricultural territory, and the majority of the continent's infrastructure. Humans cycle through institutional memory every two or three generations, which means the empire is perpetually administered by people who were not present for the decisions they are implementing. The Inquest officer at the city gate enforcing a policy established in Year 1 does not know the policy's actual purpose. He knows only that it is the policy. Most Dominion humans have no idea that Helios is sealed below the capital. The Cardinals who know have never told them. A growing moderate faction senses that something is wrong at the theological core but cannot identify what.

**Elves (High Elves / The Administration).** Population approximately 500,000. Survivors of Calidar — the elves who were outside the city when the Atlas detonated. They watched the sky go green. They lost their home, their civilization, and the Dark Elves who were their neighbors and colleagues and family. Integrated into the Dominion as an administrative caste because their bureaucratic skill and extraordinary lifespans (500-800 years, Ancient Ones 10,000+) made them invaluable. They run the empire that destroyed their civilization. They maintain sealed archives containing two pieces of information they have not published: a complete account of Calidar at its height, and documentation that Helios is a demi-god, sealed below the capital, his essence siphoned for centuries. Both files are cross-referenced under seventeen index entries the Inquest has never fully traced. They are waiting for the right moment to use what they know. They have been waiting for five hundred years. They are patient enough for this.

**Orcs (The Clans).** Population approximately 240,000. No unified government since Great Khan Morghul died in Year -45 with his succession clause unfinished. The Khanate was the most effective military force in recorded history; its fragmentation was the defining catastrophe of orcish political life. The Dominion has worked deliberately to ensure no new Khan can be legitimately named. Lifespan 300-500 years, meaning thousands of orcs alive today personally served under Morghul. They are patient in the specific way of people who have decided to wait. The suppression of Year 400 — four elders dead in imperial custody — is not a historical footnote in the steppes. It is something specific people's grandparents witnessed.

**Dwarves (The Free Holds of Stone).** Population approximately 185,000. Anarcho-syndicalist labor federation in the western mountains of Fortuna. No kings, no lords, no inherited titles. The Free Holds rejected the Helios Doctrine in Year -600 and have maintained that rejection ever since. Lifespan 300-500 years. The holds are entirely underground; the empire has no presence inside them. The empire has stopped trying to establish one. The dwarves' military capability is typically underestimated by people who survive only one encounter with it.

**Gnomes (The Collective).** Population approximately 340,000. Technocratic industrial state on the eastern archipelago of Fortuna. The Collective has been withdrawing from continental affairs since Year 120. Lifespan 200-350 years. They have assessed the Dominion as a terminal failure state since Year 120 and have been acting on that assessment, quietly, ever since. Phase Three — updated by unanimous Council vote — is what they are building toward. No non-gnome knows what it does.

**Goblins (The Resistance).** Population unknown. No central government — decentralized cells of 5-20 with no central command, no headquarters, no permanent leadership. Lifespan 30-60 years, which means the resistance sustains itself through rigorous oral tradition: each generation teaches the names of what was taken and everything attached to those names. Five hundred years of continuous guerrilla operations against the empire. The empire calls this pest control. The goblins do not expect their children to win; they expect their children to remember long enough that one generation eventually does.

**Lizard Folk (The Sects).** Population approximately 15,000 surface-visible; total unknown. Not governed as a single entity — organized into secretive knowledge sects (Astronomy, Trade Routes, Burial Rites, and others). Lifespan 600-800 years. They predicted the Atlas event ten years before it occurred. The warning went unread. They subsequently founded the Veiled Hand. They know who the lich is. They have the documentation. They have not decided when to share it or with whom.

**Cat Folk / Beast Folk (The Diaspora).** Population approximately 45,000. Stateless. No territory, no government, no recognized legal standing in imperial courts. Lifespan 60-80 years. The diaspora moves through desert trade routes and gambling towns across Fortuna, maintaining their own legal traditions through the Salt Court — a mobile governing body that has been meeting for centuries and that the empire has never successfully tracked. They carry multiple copies of their documentation papers because they expect confiscation.

### Non-Playable Race: Dark Elves

Dark Elves are not a playable race. They are a wound the world has not yet acknowledged.

They were the sub-species of the elven people who took the Dominion's theological expansionism seriously — who looked at the Helios Doctrine's five hundred years of creeping political dominance and said: this will not end well unless it is stopped. They were right. A small sect within their community reached out to their gods seeking help. The Dominion had been intercepting their communications for years. The Prophet-King sent a warrior with Heaven's Atlas. The warrior activated it in Calidar, which was also the High Elves' capital, which was why both sub-species died in the same moment.

The Dominion blamed the Dark Elves for "soliciting divine intervention against the righteous order of Fortuna" and enacted systematic policies targeting Dark Elven survivors after Year 0. These policies are documented in Inquest records as "cultural dissolution initiatives." They reduced the Dark Elven population from tens of thousands to a handful of individuals living under assumed identities.

The surviving Dark Elves are in Shadow Fen, in goblin tunnel networks, in records offices where elven administrators have quietly misclassified them as High Elves for decades. They are the most persecuted people in a world that has no shortage of persecution. They carry the memory of Calidar most personally because they were the ones who saw what was coming and could not stop it.

The Rift was created by a Dark Elven god as punishment for what the Dominion's warrior did. The ongoing existence of the rift is, in some sense, the last act of Dark Elven divine power in the world. The Dark Elves who survive know this. Most of them have mixed feelings about it.

### Geography

**Fortuna** — The continent. Called the Enlightened Continent by the Dominion. Called home by everyone else. Approximately 10,000 kilometers east to west, 12,500 north to south. The Dominion's administrative reach ends well before its declared borders. Everything beyond the fortified roads and cathedral cities is contested or uncontrolled.

**The Holy Dominion** occupies the central plains — temperate farmland, cathedral cities, fortified roads. The Cathedral District in Solara is where the Dominion's theological and administrative center sits. Below it, Helios is sealed.

**The Elven South** is dense forest southeast of the Dominion heartlands. Administered by the elven bureaucratic caste. Towering trees, sealed archives, memorial groves with no memorial tree for Calidar because there is no safe ground close enough.

**The Dwarven Mountains** run across the north. The holds are entirely underground. The surface shows rock face, sealed cave entrances, and the specific trade contact points where dwarves emerge on their own schedule.

**The Orcish Steppes** cover the west. Rolling grassland suited for horse culture, tent cities, and mounted warfare. Imperial monitoring classifies the larger encampments as "compliant seasonal settlements," which misses approximately everything.

**The Gnomish Isles** lie far to the east, past the Silver Seas. Clockwork Harbor is the port city; Mechspire is the capital inland. Access by outsiders requires permits that are almost always denied.

**Shadowfen** is a dense swamp in the southwest, classified by the empire as "uninhabitable exclusion zone." Thousands of people live here — including several of Fortuna's last surviving Dark Elves, who have found that the Veil's magic is particularly resistant to Inquest scrying.

**The Wastes of Calidar** begin south of the Elven South. Fifty miles of sickly green glass — the signature color of Heaven's Atlas's divine-organic energy — hot ash, and fluctuating magma. The interior is genuinely uninhabitable. Every elf alive carries this place in cultural memory. The surviving Dark Elves carry it more personally still.

**The Great Endless Desert** covers the central-south interior. Ancient ruins, buried cities, and the surface evidence of lizardfolk's five-thousand-year presence on this continent. The Coldwatch Observatory — surface ruins the empire records as pre-war astronomical instruments — is correct about the ruins. The observatory below them is still operational.

**The Scorched Sands** lie to the west. Naturally hostile volcanic terrain distinct from the Wastes of Calidar, though the comparison is commonly made.

**The Frostbound Reach** is the far north — tundra, ice, and displaced orcish clans driven north by Dominion suppression.

### Key Institutions

**The Holy Dominion.** A theocratic empire built on the Helios Doctrine — the claim that Helios is the chief deity of all peoples on Fortuna and that humanity is divinely mandated to govern them. The Doctrine has been maintained for five hundred years since the Atlas event by a Cardinals who know that Helios is sealed below the capital and choose to say nothing. The Magic Ban, enacted Year 1, makes all unlicensed magic punishable by imprisonment or death. The Dominion's primary enforcement arm is the Luminary Inquest.

**The Luminary Inquest.** Founded Year 1. Execution authority over unlicensed magic users. Cross-border jurisdiction across all of Fortuna. The Inquest's classified records contain evidence of the lich's true identity — the Dominion warrior who carried the Atlas, punished by a Dark Elven god, trapped in the Rift for five centuries. This evidence has not been published.

**The Veiled Hand.** Founded Year 1 by lizardfolk astronomers who watched their warning go unread. Assassination network. Anti-escalation mandate: eliminate individuals whose plans would cause another Calidar-scale catastrophe. The Hand possesses the most complete documented account of the lich's origin on Fortuna. They have not shared it with the Adventure Guild. They are still deciding whether sharing it stabilizes things or accelerates the collapse.

**The Adventure Guild.** Founded Year 450. Built on the principle that Rift containment requires a neutral institution all eight races can participate in without pledging allegiance to any nation. Ten-rank system, Stone through Relic. Extraterritorial authority in Rift zones and Guild towns. The only institution in Fortuna's history where an orc, an elf, a goblin, and a human can hold the same institutional rank. The Dominion views this as an infringement. The other seven races view it as a miracle.

**The Imperial Documentation System.** The mechanism through which the Dominion defines who exists on Fortuna and on what terms. Every resident requires papers. Paper renewal cycles differ by race: humans renew every five years, elves every twenty, orcs every three, goblins annually (in person, at an Inquest office), cat folk every six months. Papers can be confiscated. The elves who administer the system know every loophole. They use them.

### The Rift

The Rift is a spatial tear placed by a Dark Elven god at the epicenter of Heaven's Atlas's detonation and then moved, as divine punishment, to the heart of the old human capital. It manifests as an infinite dungeon that reseeds daily at midnight, managed since Year 450 by the Adventure Guild. The beings within it are near-perfect replicas of species known on the surface — but hollow: light escapes their eyes differently, they cannot speak, they sometimes twitch unnervingly or shift species mid-encounter. There is no reason or rhyme to this madness. It is the rift doing what rifts do.

On the 25th floor of the Rift, for the first time in a long time, the entity inside encountered something that was not hollow. This is documented in Guild records as a "Class-Unknown encounter event." The documentation does not include what the entity said.

What it said, to the first Guild adventurer who encountered it after centuries of hollow floor-creatures, was this: "You have eyes. Has it been long?"

The Guild's research division has been quietly investigating the implications of this report for three years. They have not published their findings. They are not sure the world is ready for their findings.
